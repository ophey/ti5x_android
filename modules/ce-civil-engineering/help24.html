STR-24 - VIERENDEEL TRUSS (two supports - horizontal loads)

The program calculates the stresses of the bending moment "M", the shear "T" and the normal stress "N" in the rods of a Vierendeel truss with rectangular mesh and two supports, subject to the action of a concentrated horizontal force applied in the upper horizontal beam.

The maximum number of the vertical rods (push-rods) is P=24.

General Description Of The Program

The typical Vierendeel truss that can be calculated with this program has the following scheme:

It is complementary to the STR-23 program of the same library module and its data archive is structured in the same way. It is thus possible to use the STR-23 program to calculate the same truss subject to vertical loads only. Then read the STR-24 program, supply it only with the value of the horizontal force "F" keeping in memory the other data already entered, and calculate the stresses in the rods due to the horizontal thrust alone.

If the force "F" is generated by an oscillating seismic thrust, the relative stresses can be added to, or subtracted from, the corresponding stresses due to the vertical forces, thus obtaining the total stress present in the same resistant section.

Each vertical push-rod is numbered progressively from p=1 to p=P starting from p=1 on the left of the truss, and its two nodes are numbered "0p" at the bottom and "1p" at the top.

The structure is therefore formed by a series of rectangular C-shaped structural mesh (in red in Figure 1), formed by one vertical push-rod on the left, of heigth "H" the same for all the push-rods, and two horizontal beams of equal length "Lp" on the right, with the left end connected to the nodes of the vertical push-rod of the mesh, and the right end at the nodes of the next push-rod.

Each mesh has the same number assigned to its vertical push-rod. The last mesh will therefore consist of only the vertical push-rod connected to the beams of the previous mesh.

The geometric and elastic characteristics of the structure are summarized in the "stiffness" of the rods, KP=J/H (vertical push-rod) and K=J/Lp (horizontal beam), with J= moment of inertia of the resistant section, H or L is the length of the structural element. The section of each rod is considered constant.

Horizontal load "F" are considered concentrated and applied only at the left end of the upper beams of the mesh "1". The sign is positive if the force "F" is directed to the rigth.

The structure is not subjected to vertical forces.

The rods are considered non-extensible, and the horizontal angular displacement of the upper nodes is the same for all the vertical rods of the truss.

The program calculates the rotation "R" of the nodes and the angular displacement "S" of the vertical rods with the Gehler-Takabeya deformation method, where R=2Eφ and S=-6Eψ, φ and ψ are the real rotation and angular displacement expressed in radiants; "E" is the elastic module of the material expressend in N/cm², and solves the system of (2P+1) equations with the Gauss-Seidel iterative method.

The iterations continue until the increment of each unknown is less than the limit "ԑ" imposed by the operator (Belluzzi - Construction Science, Vol.II/p.465, Zanichelli Ed. - Italy).

The sign of R and S, is positive if right-handed.

After having solved the system of equations (time about 1 second / node), the program, prints, for each mesh, the geometric and elastic data of its rods, the rotations R and the angular displacement "S" of its vertical rods, the bending moments "M" at the ends of its rods, the constant shear "T" and the relative normal stress "N". Finally it prints the vertical and horizontal constraint reactions in the two supports "A" to the left and "B" to the rigth.

It is also possible to print the results for a single mesh chosen by the operator, and reprint the report with data and results by pressing the SBR key twice without recalculating the truss.

Procedures For Data Input And Printing Results

The instructions for using the program are summarized in the "card emulation" similar to the magnetic card of the TI-59, which appears below the display when the APP calls the program from the library module.

To start the program and store the data of the truss Vierendeel, there are the same 3 distinct procedures respectively assigned to the A keys, B, and C keys of the calculator by the STR-23 program.

Except for the procedure [A], the other procedures can be started without a specific order.

If the new truss has different dimensions from the previous one, the operator must start procedure [A] to initialize the data archive, otherwise it will use procedures [B], and [C] to modify only the data that changes.

The stiffness "K" are expressed in cm³ with integer value between "0" and "9999". By setting "9999", the program will amplify it adequately to consider it as an infinite stiffness.

Setting a value greater than the limit "9999" allowed by the procedure, the program stops flashing this limit. In such cases, press CLR and repeat the procedure with compatible values.

The horizontal force "F" are expressed in "N" or its multiple, with integer positive or negative value.

The instructions for using the program are provided directly with the same following example shown in the STR-23 program. Finally, the stresses due to the two types of loads, vertical and horizontal, will be summed in some sections of the structure.

Example

Calculate the Vierendeel truss in figure 1 which shows the height "H" of the P = 4 vertical rods, their stiffness "KP" and the stiffness "K0" and "K1" of the horizontal beams of the 3 meshes of the structure; their length "L" and the horizontal load "F" applied in the upper beams,

The data must be stored with the procedures assigned by the program to keys A, B and C. If they have already been stored with the STR-23 program, it is only necessary to repeat the input of procedure A to set the value of the horizontal force F.

A - Start The Program

Press A: Display = previous stored value of the number of vertical rods "P" of the truss.

If it is different, enter the new value 4 and press R/S.

Display = previous stored value of their heigth "H". If it is different, enter H=120 and press R/S to store it.

Display = previous stored value of the horizontal force "F". If it is different, enter F=40000 and press R/S to store it.

Display = 0: End of procedure.

Warning:

If P≤2 or P>24, or H=0 or H>9999, the program stops and the wrong data flashes. In this cases press CLR and repeat the precedure.

B - Provide The Stiffness Of The Mesh "p"

The rods of mesh "p" are the vertical push-rod and the two beams on the rigth.

The procedure can be started for any value of "p" without a precise order.

  1. Enter push-rod number "p"=1 and press B.

    Display = previous data stored of the stiffness "KP" of the push-rod.

    If it is incorrect enter new value: 1688.

  2. Press R/S. Display = previous data stored of stiffness K0 of the lower beam.

    If it is incorrect enter new value: 781

  3. Press R/S. Display = previous data stored of stiffness K1 of the upper beam.

    If it is incorrect enter new value: 400

  4. Press R/S. Display = 2 (number of next push-rode).

    Press R/S to repeat the procedure for the push-rod "2", otherwise repeat the procedure from the beginning for another push-rod.

  5. Repeat this procedure until push-rod 4, where KP=1688 and K0 and K1=0 because they don't exist.

Warning:

If p=0 or p>P the program stops and the wrong data flashes. In this cases press CLR and repeat the precedure.

To view the data just stored for push-rod "p", repeat the procedure and correct the displayed data only if it is incorrect.

C - Provide The Length "L" Of The Beams

The procedure displays and stores the length "L" of the beams of the mesh "p" and can be started for any value of "p" without a precise order.

Enter mesh number p=1 and press C.

Display = previous data stored of the length "L".

If it is incorrect enter new value: 400 and press R/S to store it.

  • Display = 2 (number of next mesh).

    Press C or R/S to repeat the procedure for the mesh "2", otherwise repeat the procedure for another mesh.

  • Repeat this procedure until mesh p=3. (The mesh p=4 does not exist).

    E - TRUSS CALCULATION AND PRINT THE RESULTS

    If we have already memorized the load "F" (procedure "A"), the stiffnesses "K" (procedure "B"), and the lengths "L" in the beams (procedure "C"), now we can calculate the truss.

    Enter the calculation tolerance "ԑ" and press E.

    The tolerance is equal to the absolute value of the difference betwen to the value of R or S calculated in two successive iterations. It is recommended to use values between "0" (maximum precision) and "9".

    The program calculate the rotations "R" for all the nodes and the angular displacement "S" of the vertical rods, in according to Gehler-Takabeya method, and considering the movable nodes and the inextensible rods.

    Solve the system of equation with the iterative method of Gauss-Seiler, printing a "0" at the start of each new iteration.

    The iterations will end when the tolerance is respected in all nodes.

    Finally print a report with the rotations "R", the displacement "S", the bending moment M, the shear T and the normal effort N in all rods.

    At the end the display flashes the number of push-rods "P". Press CLR to stop blinking and clear the display. Warning:

    During the calculation the absolute values of the R or S must be less than 50000, otherwise the program stops flashing the calculated value.

    In this case this block can be overcome by pressing CLR and repeating the procedure "A" by setting a value of "F" divided by an appropriate "D" scale. The results must then be multiplied by the same "D" scale value.

    Or it is possible to repeat procedure "B" by setting a stiffness value amplified by an appropriate scale. The results will be less accurate but will not change scale.

    Finally, it is possible to adopt the two solutions together. How to view the report.

    The program processes the print report in the 20 column format like the original PC100-C printer from Texs Instruments. To view the print report touch the printer icon at the top center of the calculator keyboard screen.

    Since the APP displays only the last 150 lines of the report, for truss with more than 4 meshs, it is advisable to start, before calculating the truss, the "data export" function, according to the APP instructions.

    All the data printed in the video will be exported to the file named "filename.txt" chosen by the operator and stored in the Android/data/net.obry.ti5x/files/Download folder in the internal memory of the smartphone.

    To print the report again it is not necessary to recalculate the truss, but simply press the SBR key twice.

    Calculation Of The Maximum Bending Moment

    We calculate now the maximum moment acting at the base of the vertical rod of the mesh "2" just printed. We call MO=-1931 kNcm the moment due to the horizontal force "F", and MV=13580 kNcm that due to vertical loads "Q", calculated with the program STR-23.

    The total bending moment becomes M=MV+MO = 11649 kNcm.

    If "F" is an oscillating seismic force, its value will oscillate between +F and -F and the moment MO will oscillate between -1931 and +1931 kNcm.

    Consequently, the bending moment acting in the section will oscillate between MV-MO= 13580+1931= 15511 kNcm and MV-MO= 13580-1931= 11649 kNcm.

    If the beam is reinforced concrete, it must have double reinforcement, and the area AF' of the compressed reinforcement should be equal to 11649/15511= 75% of the area AF of the stretched reinforcement.

    If the section is rectangular, we can then calculate the value of the two areas AF and AF' with the STR-12 program of the same library module.
