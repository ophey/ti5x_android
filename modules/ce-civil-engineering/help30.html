STR30 - PERFECTLY INGED BEAM

The program calculates the bending moments MA and MB at the ends of a perfectly inged beam.

Different load conditions are possible, combinable in any order:

  1. uniform load "Q" along the whole beam;
  2. concentrated force "F" in the coordinate point "XF";
  3. concentrated torque "C" in the coordinate point "XC";
  4. distributed load linearly variable from Q1 to Q2, applied from the coordinate X1 to X2.

The coordinates are measured from the left node A of the beam.

The units of measurement used must be consistent and those of the International System and related multiples or submultiples. If the lengths are expressed in "m" and forces in "Newton", the load Q and the torque C must be expressed respectively in "N/m" and "Nm". The moments will therefore be expressed in "Nm".

The sign of the bending moments must respect the "Physical Convention", positive if right-handed.

The program uses only the memory registers from 90 to 99. Therefore it can be recalled from the library also during the use of the structural calculation programs from STR22 to STR27 of the lybrary-module without altering the contents of their archives.

Method Of Calculation And Formulas Used

With reference to Figure 1, called

L = length of the beam;

Q = uniform load intensity on the beam;

F = intensity of the concentrated force F applied in the XF coordinate;

C = intensity of the concentrated torque C applied in the XC coordinate,

Q1, Q2 = initial (left) and final (right) values of the intensity of the linearly variable load applied on the beam from the X1 to X2 coordinate.

MA, MB = perfect interlocking moments in "A" and "B" ends of the beamm produced by the single load;

∑MA, ∑MB = total bending moment in joints "A" and "B".

The program calculates the perfect joint moments MA and MB produced by each type of load, using the following formulas:

  1. Uniform load Q across the beam: MA= -(QL²/12); MB= (QL²/12)
  2. Concentrated force F in the XF coordinate:

    MA= F(XF/L²)(L-XF)²; MB= F(XF²/L²)(L-XF).

  3. Concentrated torque "C" in the XC coordinate:

    MA= C(1-XC/L)[2-3(1-XC/L)]; MB= C(XC/L)(2-3XC/L);

  4. Linearly distributed load from Q1 to Q2 in coordinates from X1 to X2:

    Called:

    DX= (X2-X1)/L;

    DQ= Q2-Q1;

    A1= DX/2 + X1/L;

    A2= 2DX/3 + X1/L;

    B1= (1-A1);

    B2= (1-B1);

    R = (Q1xL²/4)(DXxA1)[4(1-A1²)-DX] +

    +(DQxDXxL²/540)[270(A2-A2³)-DX²(45A2-2DX)];

    S = (Q1xL²/4)(DXxB1)[4(1-B1²)-DX] +

    +(DQxDXxL²/540)[270(B2-B2³)-DX²(45B2+2DX)];

the program calculates MA=(2S-R)/3 and MB=(2R-S)/3.

Finally the program calculates ∑MA and ∑MB as the sum of the relative MA and MB values produced by each load applied to the beam.

The physical convention of signs is used.

Instructions For Data Input And Printing Results

The instructions for using the program are summarized in the "card emulation”, similar to the magnetic card of the TI-59, which appears below the display when the APP calls the program from the library module. It summarizes the instructions assigned to each definable key, transcribed in the box referring to the key.

The data input procedures differ according to the type of load acting in the beam. Several loads of the same type can also act in the beam (see example). With the exception of the reset procedure described in point 1, the others can be performed without a specific order.

  1. RESET DATA-ARCHIVE Enter "L" and press SBRRST.

    The program reset the values of loads and their coordinates stored for a previous calculation.

    At the end the display show "0".

    In addition the program print the title of the program "STR30" and the value of the length "L" of the beam.

    To see the report in the phone video, tap the printer icon at the top center of the app keyboard and scroll the displayed image. To return to the app keyboard tap the "<" icon at the bottom left or rigth of the phone screen.

  2. Set Uniform Load "Q" And Length "L":
    1. Press Akey. Display = stored value of "Q". If it is incorrect, enter the new value.

      Press R/Sto store "Q" and display "L" stored value. If it is incorrect, enter the new value;

      Press R/Sto store "L". The program displays the moment "MA" due to the load "Q".

      By continuing to press R/S, "MB" and "MA" will be displayed alternately.

    2. Stores MA and MB and display "∑MA" and "∑MB":

      Having calculated and displayed the moments MA and MB due to the load Q on the beam, press Ekey to add them to the overall moments ∑MA and ∑MB rispectively, already existing in the joints of the beam.

      At the end the program displays the new value of ∑MA. By continuing to press R/S, "∑MB" and "∑MA" will be displayed alternately.

      In addition the program prints the data of the load "Q", the length "L" of the beam and the moments "MA" and "MB" produced by this load in the joints.

    3. Notes:

      To provide data for another similar load, repeat the procedure correcting only the data that changes.

      To correct the load and/or its coordinate before performing step 2, repeat step 1.

      To correct ∑MA and ∑MB after performing step 2, repeat step 1 and 2 changing only the sign of "Q". Then repeat the procedure with the new value of "Q" and "L".

  3. Set Concentrated Force "F" And Its "XF" Coordinate:
    1. Press Bkey. Diplay shows the stored value of "F". If it is incorrect, enter the new value.

      Press R/Sto store "F" and display "XF" stored value. If it is incorrect, enter the new value

      Press R/Sto store "XF". The program displays the bending moment "MA" due to the force "F".

      By continuing to press R/S, "MB" and "MA" will be displayed alternately.

    2. Stores MA and MB and display "∑MA" and "∑MB":

      Press Ekey to add MA and MB results to the overall moments ∑MA and ∑MB rispectively.

      Follow the instructions of the previous step 2.

  4. Set Concentrated Torque "C" And Its "XC" Coordinate:
    1. Press Ckey. Diplay shows the stored value of "F". If it is incorrect, enter the new value.

      Press R/Sto store "F" and display "XF" stored value. If it is incorrect, enter the new value

      Press R/Sto store "XF". The program displays the bending moment "MA" due to the force "F".

      By continuing to press R/S, "MB" and "MA" will be displayed alternately.

    2. Stores MA and MB and display "∑MA" and "∑MB":

      Press Ekey to add MA and MB results to the overall moments ∑MA and ∑MB rispectively.

      Follow the instructions of the previous step 2.

  5. Set Linear Distributed Load From "Q1" To "Q2" And Its Coordinates:
    1. Press Dkey. Diplay shows the stored value of "Q1". If it is incorrect, enter the new value.

      Press R/Sto display "X1". If it is incorrect, enter the new value.

      Press R/Sto diplay the stored value of "Q2". If it is incorrect, enter the new value.

      Press R/Sto display "X2". If it is incorrect, enter the new value.

      Press R/Sto store it.

      At the end display shows the bending moment "MA" due to the linear distributed load "Q1-Q2".

      By continuing to press R/S, "MB" and "MA" will be displayed alternately.

    2. Stores MA and MB and display "∑MA" and "∑MB":

      Press Ekey to add MA and MB results to ∑MA and ∑MB rispectively.

      Follow the instructions of the previous step 2.

  6. Display And Print The Results "∑MA" and "∑MB"
    1. Press SBR =keys to complete the printing of the report with the final values of ∑MA and ∑MB in the joints of the beam.

      At the end the program display the bending moment "∑MA". By continuing to press R/S, "∑MB" and "∑MA" will be displayed alternately.

Warning

If the new beam has loads and/or dimensions different from the previous one, the operator can only modify the data that change otherwise, he must first reset all the data with the previous procedure "1".

For loads Q, F and C, the program accepts integer values, positive or negative, less than "500'000" in absolute value. The length "L" of the beam and the coordinates of the loads must be integer, positive and less than "10'000". Otherwise the program stops flashing the limit value not allowed. In this case, press CLR to eliminate the blinking and repeat the procedure.

The program eliminates any decimal digit from the load value, and the decimal digits beyond the second from the coordinates value.

To view the print results report, touch the printer icon at the top center of the app's keyboard.

      ∑MA = -2041,666667 -1087.117347 -821,647959 = -3950.14881dNm

      Given the symmetry of the loads applied symmetrically along the beam, the two values of the final moments in the joints are equal and opposite.

      ∑MA is negative and left-handed. It then stretches the upper fibers of the beam section. ∑MB is positive rigth-handed and therefore stretches the upper fibers like MA.

      According to the convention of the signs of the Science of Constructions, both moments are negative because they stretch the upper fibers.
