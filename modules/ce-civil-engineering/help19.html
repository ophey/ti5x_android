STR-19 - Distribution of the Seismic Shear Force

The program calculates the distribution coefficients of the seismic shear force in the pillars of an infinitely rigid planar building structure, in the two directions X and Y and as a function of their stiffness and the eccentricity between the center of gravity GW of the masses and GK of the stiffnesses.

The maximum number of pillars in the structure is 37.

The value of the seismic vertical load "W" present in each pillar must be positive and less than 10⁵.

The sign of the pillar coordinates must be positive in both "X" and "Y" directions and less than 10⁴.

The sign of the stiffnesses "K" of the pillars, in both directions, must be positive and less than 10⁶.

The units of measurement must be: lengths in meters, forces in Newton and related multiples or submultiples.

Symbology

  • N = number of pillars (max 37)
  • Wn = seismic vertical load on pillar "n"
  • Xn, Yn = coordinates of pillar "n"
  • KXn, KYn = stiffness of pillar "n" in X and Y direction
  • XG, YG = coordinates of the center of the gravity GW of the seismic masses
  • XK, YK = coordinates of the center of the gravity GK of the stiffnesses
  • CXn, CYn = distribution coefficients of the seismic shear force in pillar "n" neglecting the torsion effect;
  • EXn, EYn = distribution coefficients of the seismic shear force in pillar "n" considering the torsion effect;

Instructions

The instructions for using the program are summarized in the "card emulation” similar to the magnetic card of the TI-59, which appears below the display when the APP calls the program from the library module.

It summarizes the instructions assigned to each definable key, transcribed in the box referring to the key. Each input procedure is independent and the operator can perform it without a predetermined order.

The program calculates the coefficients Cn and En of the generic pillar "n" for a structure with a maximum of N = 37 pillars.

To start the program, enter N and press the SBR CLR keys.

The data of the pillar "n" are: the "seismic" load Wn, the coordinates Xn and Yn of the center of gravity of the column section, the stiffnesses KXn and KYn of the pillars, measured in both directions.

To reduce the time required for storing all data of the pillars, the program stores the 5 data in 5 temporary registers, each managed by the definable key from A to E as shown in the card. The operator can thus view the data and, if necessary, correct it.

After completing the input of the 5 data, the operator will archive them with the procedure assigned to key 2nd B'.

For another pillar, it will be sufficient to replace only the data that change and repeat the archiving for the new pillar.

With the procedure assigned to key 2nd A' it is possible, at any time, to recall the data of a generic "r" pillar in the 5 provisional registers, correct them and assign them to the same or another pillar by archiving them with procedure 2nd B'.

After storing the data of all the pillars of the structure, with the procedure assigned to key 2nd C' it is possible to view in order, by pressing R/S, the "total seismic weight" of the structure, the XG and YG coordinates of the center of gravity of the seismic masses and the XK and YK coordinates of the center of gravity of the stiffnesses.

Using the procedure assigned to the definable key 2nd D', the operator can view, for a generic pillar "n", the distribution coefficients of the shear force due to the "total seismic weight", in order, by pressing R/S, CX, EX, CY, EY.

Pressing 2nd E', the program print data and results for all pillars.

The instructions for using the program will be illustrated directly with an example.

Example

Calculate the coefficients for the structure shown in the previous figure.

  1. Initialization:

    Enter total number of pillars "N", 8 and press SBR CLR.

  2. Provide the Data of the Pillars:

    Each input procedure is independent and each pillar can be stored without a specific order.

    • Pillar "1":
      1. Enter data W=3600, X=20, Y=15, KX=400, KY=225.

        Set "W": press A; display = previoulsy stored data.
        Enter 3600, press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "X": press B; display = previoulsy stored data.
        Enter 20 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "Y" : press C; display = previoulsy stored data.
        Enter 15 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "KX" : press D; display = previoulsy stored data.
        Enter 400 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "KY" : press E; display = previoulsy stored data.
        Enter 225 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

      2. Archive pillar "1" data:

        Enter 1, press 2nd B'.

        The program will compact and archive the 5 pillar data.

        At the end display 0. End of procedure.

    • Pillar "2":
      1. Enter data W=7200, X=580, Y=15, KX=400, KY=225.

        Store only data that changes from the previous pillar.

        We have already stored the 5 data of pillar 1 and therefore we will only replace W and X.

        Set "W": press A; display = 3600 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 7200 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "X" : press B; display = 20 (incorrect stored data).
        Enter 580 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        To check one of the other 3 data, for example KX, press D; display = 400
        If the data is incorrect, enter new value and press R/S to store the new data.

      2. Archive pillar "2" data:
        Enter 2 and press 2nd B'.

        The program will compact and archive the 5 pillar data.

        At the end display 0. End of procedure.


    • Pillar "3":
      1. Enter data W=6000, X=1380, Y=15, KX=400, KY=225.

        Even for this pillar we will only change W and X with the same procedure.

        Set "X": press B; display = 580 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 1380 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "W" : press A; display = 7200 (incorrect stored data).
        Enter 6000 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

      2. Archive pillar "3" data:

        Enter 3 and press 2nd B'.

        The program will compact and archive the 5 data of the pillar "3".

        At the end display 0. End of procedure.


    • Pillar "6":
      1. Enter data W=6000, X=1380, Y=585, KX=400, KY=225.

        The pillar has the same data as already stored pillar 3, except the Y coordinate only will change this.

        Set "Y": press C; display = 15 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 580 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

      2. Archive pillar "6" data:

        Enter 6 and press 2nd B'.

        The program will compact and archive the 5 data of the pillar "6".

        At the end display 0. End of procedure.


    • Pillar "4":
      1. Enter data W=6000, X=15, Y=580, KX=225, KY=400.

        Comparing these data with those of pillar 6, just stored, we will change X, Y, KX and KY.

        Set "X": press B; display = 1380 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 15 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "Y": press C; display = 585 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 580 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "KX": press D; display = 400 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 225 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "KY": press E; display = 225 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 400 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

      2. Archive pillar "4" data:

        Enter 4 and press 2nd B'.

        The program will compact and archive the 5 pillar data.

        At the end display 0. End of procedure.


    • Pillar "5":
      1. Enter data W=9600, X=585, Y=580, KX=225, KY=400.

        Comparing these data with those of pillar 4, just stored, we will change W and X.

        Set "X": press B; display = 15 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 585 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "W": press A; display = 6000 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 9600 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Check "KY": press E; display = 400 correct data.
        End of procedure.

      2. Archive pillar "5" data:

        Enter 5 and press 2nd B'.

        The program will compact and archive the data of pillar "5".

        At the end display 0. End of procedure.


    • Pillar "7":

      Comparing the data of the pillar "7" and pillar "4", already archived, we will only change W and Y.

      1. Retrieve the pillar "4" data from the archive:

        Enter 4 and press 2nd A'.

        The program unpacks the W, X, Y, KX, KY data of pillar "4" from the archive and stores them in the relative registers assigned to keys A, B, C, D, E.

        At the end display 0.

      2. Enter data W=3600, X=15, Y=1080, KX=225, KY=400.

        Set "y": press C; display = 580 stored data Y4.
        Enter 1080 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Set "W": press A; display = 6000 stored data W4.

        Enter 3600 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

        Check "X": press B; display = 15 correct data.
        End of procedure.

      3. Archive pillar "7" data:

        Enter 7 and press 2nd B'.

        The program will compact and archive the data of pillar "7".

        At the end display 0. End of procedure.


    • Pillar "8":
      1. Enter data W=3600, X=585, Y=1080, KX=225, KY=400.

        Comparing these data with those of pillar 7, just stored, we will change only X.

        Set "X": press B; display = 15 incorrect stored data.
        Enter 585 press R/S; display = 0 (end of procedure).

      2. Archive pillar "8" data:

        Enter 8 and press 2nd B'.

        The program will compact and archive the data of pillar "8".

        At the end display 0. End of procedure.


  3. Results:
    • Displays XG, YG, XK, YK coordinates:

      Pressing 2nd C', the program calculates the total seismic mass ΣW and the coordinates XG, YG of the center of gravity of the masses, and the coordinates XK, YK of the center of gravity of the stiffnesses.

      At the end displays ΣW = 45600 .

      Pressing in sequence R/S display the coordinates XG, YG, XK, YK and repeat the sequence.

    • Displays CX, EX, CY, EY of the pillar "n"

      Enter "n" and pressing 2ndD'.

      The program calculates CXn, EXn, CYn, and EYn distribution coefficients of the seismic shear force in pillar "n" neglecting or considering the torsion effect.

      At the end displays "n+1".

      Pillar "1":

      Enter "1" and press 2ndD'.

      Display CX = 0.09.

      Pressing in sequence R/S display EX, CY, EY.

      Pressing again R/S display "n+1" = 2.

      Pressing again R/S or 2nd D' repeat the procedure for the next pillar.

      Same procedure for the other pillars.

  4. Print data and results for all pillars:

    Press 2ndE'.

    About The Program

    Number of steps: from 000 to 692.

    Registers used: from 00 to 25 and from 26 to 25+2xN

    Flags used: 1

    Labels: Nop, CLR, SUM, Prt, INV, Op, RCL, Adv, STO, Dsz, If Flg, =, - , + , and definable keys.
