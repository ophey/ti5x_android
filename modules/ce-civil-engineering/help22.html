STR-22 - FRAMES

The program calculates the bending moments at the ends of the rods of a rectangular mesh frame, subject to the action of any type of vertical forces and horizontal forces, positive or negative, concentrated at the level of the individual floors.

The operator can choose to consider the frame with movable or fixed nodes or shear-type.

General Description Of The Program

The scheme of the typical calculable frame is the following:

The nodes must be numbered from left to right and from bottom to top; the planes from the bottom to top; each beam is named with the number of its node on the left; each pillar with its node number at the top.

If N=number of nodes and P=number of floors, the program can calculate the frame if (2N+P)≤80. It will be possible to calculate a frame with C=7 pillars and N=5 floors, or C=2 pillars and N=16 floors, or a frame with N=1 floor e max C=39 pillars. The maximum values almost double if the frame is symmetrical and symmetrically loaded. (See the followiong examples).

The geometric and elastic characteristics of the structure are summarized in the "stiffness" of the pillars and beams, K=J/L, with J= moment of inertia of the resistant section, and L= length of the structural element. The section of each pillar or beam is considered constant.

Any missing rod must always be considered present in the frame and connected to the respective nodes but with stiffness K=0. A missing pillar is equivalent to a simple support of the upper beam.

The presence of cantilever beams is allowed in each floor, to the left of the initial pillar and to the right of the final pillar. The cantilever must always be considered with stiffness K=0. (see example 2).

The vertical loads are considered to be present only in the beams, and are entered with the perfect joint moments on the left (MA) and on the right (MB) of each node "n".

The pillars must not have horizontal loads applied along their axis.

The action of the seismic forces in the "p" plan is given with the seismic moments MS= ΣFx(H/3). "ΣF" is the sum of the seismic concentrated forces "F" agents from the "p" plan to the top "P" plan, positive if directed to the right. "H" is the height of the "p" plan with respect to the lower plan.

The sign of the bending moment, according to the physical-mathematical convention, is positive if right-handed.

The program calculates the rotation "R" and the angular displacement "S" of the frame nodes with the Gheler-Takabeya method, and solves the system of (N+P) equations with the Gauss-Seidel iterative method. The iterations continue until the increment of each unknown is zero (Belluzzi - Construction Science, Vol.II/p.465, Zanichelli Ed. - Italy).

After solving the system of equations (time about 1 sec./node), the program calculates in each node, the bending moments at the ends of the lower pillar and of the right beam of each node, and prints the results for all nodes. It is also possible to print the results for a single node chosen by the operator.

Several examples illustrate how to calculate the frame with nodes free to rotate and move, or rotate without moving (fixed nodes), or move without rotating (shear-type frame), also considering the influence of the foundation beams.

Or how to calculate a continuous beam with free or wedged ends. Or to calculate only the bending moments due to vertical loads (moments MV) and those due only to seismic loads (moments MS) in the different directions of the forces at the various planes according to the vibration mode of the structure, and finally calculate the total bending moment by sum effects (MV + MS) or (MV-MS).

By assigning particular values to the stiffness of the beams and pillars, it is possible to solve symmetrical and symmetrically loaded frames, with the number of nodes greater than the limit allowed by the program.

Procedures For Data Input And Printing Results

The instructions for using the program are summarized in the "card emulation" similar to the magnetic card of the TI-59, which appears below the display when the APP calls the program from the library module.

To start the program and store the data of the frame, there are 4 distinct procedures respectively assigned to the A key, and B, C, D keys of the calculator.

Except for the procedure [A], the other procedures can be started without a specific order.

If the new frame has different dimensions from the previous one, the operator must start procedure [A] to initialize the data archive, otherwise it will use procedures [B], [C] and [D] to modify only the data that changes.

The stiffness "K" are expressed in cm³ with integer value between 0 and 9999. By setting "9999", the program will amplify it adequately to consider it as an infinite stiffness.

The moments are expressed in "kNcm" with integer value berween -49999 and +49999.

Setting a value greater than the limit allowed by the procedure, the program stops flashing this limit. In such cases, press CLR and repeat the procedure with compatible values.

The instructions for using the program are supplied directly with the following.

EXAMPLE 1

Calculate the frame illustrated in figure 1 considering it with movable nodes. The stiffness of the rods, the moments in the nodes and the seismic moments are indicated in the following figure 2 and must be stored with the input procedures "A", "B", "C" and "D" of the program.

A - Start The Program

Press A: Display = previous stored value of of the number of pillars "C" on the ground floor.

Enter the new value 4 and press R/S. Display = previous stored value of "P" (number of floors).

Enter P=2 and press R/S. Display = 18: total number of registers used for the archive. It is less than 80, so the frame can be calculated. End of procedure.

B - PROVIDE THE STIFFNESS OF THE RODS OF NODE "n"

The members of node "n" are the lower pillar and the beam to the right of the node.

The procedure can be started for any value of "n" without a precise order.

  1. Enter node number 1 and press B.

    Display = previous data stored of the stiffness "KP" of the pillar under the node.

    If it is incorrect enter new value: 169.

  2. Press R/S. Display = previous data stored of stiffness KT of the beam to the right of the node.

    If it is incorrect enter new value: 781

  3. Press R/Sit. Display = 2 (number of next node).

    Press R/S to repeat the procedure for the next node, otherwise repeat the procedure from the beginning for another node.

  4. Repeat this procedure until node 8, where KT=0 being an cantilever.

Warning:

If n=0 or n>N the program stops and flash "0" or "N". In this cases press CLR and repeat the precedure.

To view the data just stored for node "n", repeat the procedure and correct the displayed data only if it is incorrect.

C - Provide The Moments "IMR" And "IML" To The Left And Right Of The Node "n"

The procedure can be started for any value of "n" without a precise order.

  1. Enter node number 1 and press C.

    Display = previous data stored of "IMR" to the left of the node.

    If it is incorrect (the cantilever to the left of this node does not exist), enter new value: 0.

  2. Press R/S to display previous data stored of "IML" to the right of the node.

    If it is incorrect enter new value 2000 [+/-] and press R/S to store it.

  3. Display = 2 (number of next node).

    Press C or R/S to repeat the procedure for the next node, otherwise repeat the procedure for another node.

  4. Repeat this procedure until node 8, where IML=0 being a cantilever not subject to any load.

D - Provide The Seismic Moment "MP" In The Floor "p"

The procedure can be started for any value of "p" without a precise order.

  1. Enter floor number "p"= 1 and press D. Display = previous data stored of MP1. If it is incorrect enter new value 2667.
  2. Press R/S to store it. Display = 2 (number of the upper floor).
  3. Press R/S or D to provide the seismic moment in the floor "2". Display = previous data stored of MP2. If it is incorrect enter new value 1067.
  4. Press R/S to store it. Display = 3 (number of the upper floor).

    Floor 3 does not exist. So press CLR (end of procedure).

Verification of the stored data

Now we verify the accuracy of the moment "MP" just stored on floor "1"

Enter 1 and press D.

Display MP=1067. This is correct.

Now we can check other data already stored by executing the relative procedure.

E - Frame Calculation And Print Result For All Nodes

If we have already memorized all the seismic moments "Ms" (procedure "D"), the stiffnesses "K" (procedure "B") and the moments "MI" (procedure "C"), now we can calculate the frame.

Pressing E the program calculate the rotations "R" for all the nodes and the angular displacement "S" for all the floors, in according to Gehler-Takabeya method, and considering the movable nodes. Finally calculated the moments at the ends of the rods and print, for all nodes, a report, with the rotations "R", the displacement "S", and the moments in the pillar under the node and in the beam to the right.

At the end the program display 0.

Warning:

The absolute values of the rotations and displacements must be less than 50000, otherwise the program stops flashing the calculated value.

How to view the node report.

The program processes the print report in the 20 column format like the original PC100-C printer from Texs Instruments. To view the print report touch the printer icon at the top center of the calculator keyboard screen.

Since the APP displays only the last 150 lines of the report, for frames with more than 10 nodes, it is advisable to start, before calculating the frame, the "data export" function, according to the APP instructions.

All the data printed in the video will be exported to the file named "filename.txt" chosen by the operator and stored in the Android/data/net.obry.ti5x/files/Download folder in the internal memory of the smartphone.

E - Calculation Of The Frame With Fixed Nodes

With this procedure, the program calculates the frame considering the nodes free to rotate but not to move horizontally.

For the frame just calculated it is not necessary to correct any data. Therefore we can immediately calculate the frame again.

Press 2nd St Flg 1 to select the calculation as frame "with fixed nodes".

Press E to start the new calculation of the "R" rotations of all nodes.

Finally, after a few seconds, it reset flag 1 and stops processing by displaying "0".

To view the report with the results of all nodes, tap the printer icon in the top center of the calculator screen.

We can immediately note that in all floors the displacement "S" is zero. Therefore the seismic moment must be absorbed by a horizontal support. The one on floor 2 must absorb a force of F2 = 3MP2/H2 = 3x1067 / 320 = 10 kN. The support on floor 1 has a force F1 = 3x2667/400 - F2 = 10kN.

In the pillars of floor 1, the bending moment MB is equal to 50% of the moment at the top MH.

The equilibrium of each node is also satisfied since the sum of the moments converging in them, gives a result comparable to zero.

E Key - Calculation Of The Frame As "SHEAR-TYPE"

With this procedure, the program calculates the frame considering it as "shear-type", with the nodes free to move but not to rotate.

For the frame just calculated it is not necessary to correct any data. Therefore we can immediately calculate the frame again.

Press 2nd St Flg 2 to select the calculation as frame "shear-type".

Press E to start the new calculation.

At the end it reset flag 2 and stops processing by displaying "0".

To view the report with the results of all nodes, tap the printer icon in the top center of the calculator screen.

The results are shown in the following figure 5.

We can immediately note that in all nodes the rotation "R" is zero.

All the beams, being considered non-deformable and with infinite stiffness, keep the stress of the perfect joint moment at the extremes.

The displacement S of the floor is opposed only by the deformation of the pillars. The seismic moment MP is distributed in the pillars in proportion to their stiffness. Since they have equal stiffness, they are stressed at the extremes with the same bending moment. The shear along the pillars is constant.

About The Program

Number of steps: from 953.

Registers used: from 00 to 89

Flags used: from 0 to 3
