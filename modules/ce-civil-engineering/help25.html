STR-25 - CANTILEVERED VIERENDEEL TRUSS

The program calculates the stresses of the bending moment "M", the shear "T" and the normal stress "N" in the rods of a cantilevered Vierendeel truss with rectangular mesh on two joints, subject to the action of uniform loads in the lower and upper beams.

The vertical rods are "push rods" and their maximum number is P=25.

General Description Of The Program

The scheme of the typical calculable Vierendeel truss is the following:

Each vertical push-rod is numbered progressively from p=1 to p=P starting from p=1 on the left of the truss, and its two nodes are numbered "0p" at the bottom and "1p" at the top.

The structure is therefore formed by a series of reverse C-shaped structural mesh (in red in Figure 1), formed by a vertical push-rod on the right and two horizontal beams of equal length connected at the right end to the push-rod of the mesh and at the left end to the push-rod of the previous mesh. The beams of mesh 1, at the left end, are perfectly interlocked. The height of the push-rods is the same in all mesh. Each mesh has the same number assigned to its vertical push-rod.

The geometric and elastic characteristics of the structure are summarized in the "stiffness" of the rods, KP=J/H (vertical push-rod) and K=J/Lp (horizontal beam), with J= moment of inertia of the resistant section, H or L is the length of the structural element. The section of each rod is considered constant.

Vertical loads are considered to be present only in beams:

Q0 in the lower beam "0"; Q1 in the upper beam "1" of the mesh "p".

The structure is not subjected to horizontal forces.

The program calculates the rotation "R" of the nodes and the angular displacement "S" of the two beams of each mesh with the Gehler-Takabeya method, and solves the system of (3P) equations with the Gauss-Seidel iterative method. The iterations continue until the increment of each unknown is less than the limit "ԑ" imposed by the operator (Belluzzi - Construction Science, Vol.II/p.465, Zanichelli Ed. - Italy).

The sign of R and S, is positive if right-handed.

After having solved the system of equations (time about 1 second / node), the program, by pressing the SBR key twice, prints, for each mesh, the geometric and elastic data of its rods, the rotations R of the nodes and the angular displacement "S" of its two beams on the right, the bending moments "M" and the shear "T" at the ends of its rods, the relative normal stress "N" and the constraint reactions in the two vertical (V) and horizontal (H) components.

It is also possible to print the results for a single mesh chosen by the operator.

An example illustrate how to calculate the Vierendeel truss.

Procedures For Data Input And Printing Results

The instructions for using the program are summarized in the "card emulation" similar to the magnetic card of the TI-59, which appears below the display when the APP calls the program from the library module.

To start the program and store the data of the truss Vierendeel, there are 3 distinct procedures respectively assigned to the A key, B, and C keys of the calculator.

Except for the procedure [A], the other procedures can be started without a specific order.

If the new truss has different dimensions from the previous one, the operator must start procedure [A] to initialize the data archive, otherwise it will use procedures [B], and [C] to modify only the data that changes.

The stiffness "K" are expressed in cm³ with integer value between "1" and "9999". By setting "9999", the program will amplify it adequately to consider it as an infinite stiffness.

The load "Q" in the beams are expressed in "N/cm" and "L" or "H" in cm, with integer value between "1" and "9999".

Setting a value "0" or greater than the limit "9999" allowed by the procedure, the program stops flashing this limit. In such cases, press CLR and repeat the procedure with compatible values.

The instructions for using the program are supplied directly with the following example.

Example

Calculate the Vierendeel truss illustrated in figure 1. The heigth "H" of the P=3 vertical push-rods; the stiffness "KP" of the push-rod and "K0" and "K1" of the horizontal beams of the mesh "p"; the length "L" and loads "Q0" and "Q1" in the beams, are indicated in the following figure 2 and must be stored with the input procedures "A", "B" and "C" of the program.

A - Start The Program

Press A: Display = previous stored value of the number of vertical rods "P" of the truss.

Enter the new value 3 and press R/S. Display = previous stored value of their heigth "H".

Enter H=80 cm and press R/S. Display = 0: End of procedure. Warning:

If P=0 or P>25, or H=0 or H>9999, the program stops and flash "0" or "P" or "9999".

In this cases press CLR and repeat the precedure.

B - Set The Stiffness Of The Mesh "p"

The rods of mesh "p" are the vertical push-rod and the two beams on the left.

The procedure can be started for any value of "p" without a precise order.

  1. Enter push-rod number "p"=1 and press B.

    Display = previous data stored of the stiffness "KP" of the push-rod.

    If it is incorrect enter new value: 844.

  2. Press R/S. Display = previous data stored of stiffness K0 of the lower beam.

    If it is incorrect enter new value: 3125

  3. Press R/S. Display = previous data stored of stiffness K1 of the upper beam.

    If it is incorrect enter new value: 675

  4. Press R/Sit. Display = 2 (number of next push-rode).

    Press R/S to repeat the procedure for the push-rod "2", otherwise repeat the procedure from the beginning for another push-rod.

  5. Repeat this procedure until push-rod 3, where KP = 844, K0 and K1 = 844.
Warning:

If p=0 or p>P the program stops and flash "0" or "P". In this cases press CLR and repeat the precedure.

To view the data just stored for push-rod "p", repeat the procedure and correct the displayed data only if it is incorrect.

C - Set The Length "L" And Uniform Loads "Q"

The procedure displays and stores the length "L" of the beams of the mesh "p" and the related uniform loads "Q0" and Q1 and can be started for any value of "p" without a precise order.

  1. Enter mesh number p=1 and press C.

    Display = previous data stored of the length "L".

    If it is incorrect enter new value: 100.

  2. Press R/S to display previous data stored of "Q0"

    If it is incorrect enter new value 100

  3. Press R/S to store it and display previous data stored of "Q1"

    If it is incorrect enter new value 80 and press R/S to store it.

    warning: An incorrect data was intentionally entered.

  4. Display = 2 (number of next mesh).

    Press C or R/S to repeat the procedure for the mesh "2", otherwise repeat the procedure for another mesh.

  5. Repeat this procedure until mesh p=3.
Verification of the stored data

Now we verify the accuracy of the loads already stored for the mesh p=1

  1. Enter push-rod number "p"=1 and press B.

    Display = previous data stored of the length "L": "100"

    It is correct.

  2. Press R/S. Display = Q0= "100".

    It is correct.

  3. Press R/S. Display = Q1= "80" of the upper beam.

    It is incorrect. Then enter new value: 90.

  4. Press R/S to replace it. Display = 2 (number of next push-rode).

    Press R/S to repeat the procedure for the push-rod "2", otherwise we can check other data already stored by executing the relative procedure.

E - Truss Calculation And Print The Results

If we have already memorized the stiffnesses "K" (procedure "B"), the lengths "L" and the loads "Q" in the beams (procedure "C"), now we can calculate the truss.

Enter the calculation tolerance "ε" and press E.

The tolerance is equal to the absolute value of the difference betwen to the value of R or S calculated in two successive iterations. It is recommended to use values between "0" (maximum precision) and "9".

The program calculate the rotations "R" for all the nodes and the angular displacement "S" of the two beams of all meshs, in according to Gehler-Takabeya method, and considering the movable nodes and the inextensible rods.

Solve the system of equation with the iterative method of Gauss-Seiler, printing a "0" at the start of each new iteration.

The iterations will end when the tolerance is respected in all nodes.

Finally print a report with the rotations "R", the displacement "S", the bending moment M, the shear T and the normal effort N in all rods.

At the end the display flashes "0". Press CLR to stop blinking and clear the display.

Warning:

During the calculation the absolute values of the R or S must be less than 50000, otherwise the program stops flashing the calculated value.In this case, press CLR to stop flashing, repeat the input of the stiffnesses (procedure B) with values proportionally increased by an appropriate scale and recalculate the structure with procedure E.

How to view the report.

The program processes the print report in the 20 column format like the original PC100-C printer from Texs Instruments. To view the print report touch the printer icon at the top center of the calculator keyboard screen.

Since the APP displays only the last 150 lines of the report, for truss with more than 4 meshs, it is advisable to start, before calculating the truss, the "data export" function, according to the APP instructions.

All the data printed in the video will be exported to the file named "filename.txt" chosen by the operator and stored in the Android/data/net.obry.ti5x/files/Download folder in the internal memory of the smartphone.

To print the report again it is not necessary to recalculate the truss, but simply press the SBR key twice.

About The Program

Number of steps: from 944.

Registers used: from 00 to 89

Flags used: 0, 1
