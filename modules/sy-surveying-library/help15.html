This program calculates horizontal distance to and elevation at the rod station using stadia methods, given the instrument elevation and height, readings, and angle. The program may be sued to detail a fixed station, or to perform a traverse. In the case of a traverse, the program keeps track of station numbers, and transfers the calculated elevation at the rod to the proper register for use as the base elevation of the next leg, and the elevation closure error is determined and balanced at each station.

  • Select the degree mode, press 2nd Deg
  • Initialize, press 2nd E'
  • Enter reference elevation, press A
  • Enter instrument height, press B
  • Enter stadia interval, press C
  • Enter vertical or zenith angle:
    • Vertical, press D
    • Zenith, press 9 0 - angle = D
  • Enter rod reading, press E
  • Compute horizontal distance, press 2nd A'
  • Compute change in elevation, press 2nd B'
  • Compute elevation of next station, press R/S

For another reduction from the same point, enter new stadia interval above, otherwise continue below.

  • Display current station # and occupy next station, press 2nd C'

Restart above to enter instrument height for next station, otherwise continue below to distribute closure error.

  • Enter computed elevation of station, press 2nd D'
  • Enter Hz Dist to station and compute corrected elevation, press R/S

Repeat those last two steps for each leg in the same order as traversed.

Registers used: 01-08, 10, 11, 13-18
