This program simulates landing a spacecraft on the Martian soil. When you take control, the spacecraft is at an altitude of 2603 feet with a downward velocity of 487 ft/src. These conditions are displayed as -487.2603. The object is to make a perfect landing; that is, land with a final velocity of zero at altitude zero. To accomplish this task, you have 630 units of fuel to burn in one-second bursts. Each burn must be 0 to 65 units. Initially each unit of fuel provides an acceleration of 1 ft/sec away from the surface. For realism, the effect of the burn changes as the fuel is consumed and the mass of the spacecraft changes. Gravitation of Mars is assumed to be 13 fr/sec.

  • Initialize program : SBR CLR
  • Press E, -487.2603 will be displayed as the initial status of velocity and altitude.
  • Key in amount of burn (0 to 75), press A. The revised status will be displayed.
  • Repeat step above until you land or crash the spacecraft. A perfect landing will be displayed as 0. A number flashing in the display or printed with a question mark is the velocity at which you crashed.
  • Press C at any time to get the current status.
