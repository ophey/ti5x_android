In many circumstances the set of numbers which measure some characteristic of a set of objects under study are said to be normally distributed. That is, a plot of these numbers against their number of occurrences would follow the normal curve, with a peak at the mean (average value) of these numbers.

This means that knowing only x, we can calculate Z(x) at that point. And because we have an equation for the curve we can approximate the area needed to a high degree of accuracy using numerical techniques. The area Q(x) is calculated by the program. The area to the left of x is called P(x) and is found using the equation:

P(x) = 1 - Q(x)

A
Enter x, computes the height of the normal curve at x.
B
Computes the area under the normal curve to the right of the last-entered x (A must be done first).
