CL-15 — Random Number Generator

This program provides functions to generate random numbers uniformly-distributed over a specified interval, or normally-distributed with a specified mean and standard deviation. Note this implementation does not use low-quality linear-congruential pseudorandom number generation; instead, it relies on a cryptographically-strong algorithm seeded by system entropy.

First of all, press 2nd E´ to initialize statistics gathering.

Then, enter the parameters for the random variable: for a uniform distribution,

  • lowbound A
  • highbound B

or, for a normal distribution:

  • mean A
  • stddev B

Then, press C to generate the next uniformly-distributed random number, or press 2nd C' to generate the next normally-distributed number.

After having generated a few numbers, you can examine their statistics with the usual functions: 2nd mean(x) to show their mean, and INV 2nd mean(x) to see their standard deviation. RCL 03 shows how many numbers you have generated.

Registers used: 01-06, 09-11.
