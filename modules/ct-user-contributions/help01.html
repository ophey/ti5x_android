JS-01 — Type and standard equation of conic sections

The program determines what the quadratic equation represents and then, only for non-degenerate conics, canonical or standard form is obtained.

Type and standard form of quadratic equations given the general quadratic equation:

ax2 + bxy + cy2 + dx + ey + f = 0

Make sure the calculator is in degree mode, if not, press 2nd DEG (by default the emulator is in DEG).

  • Enter the coefficient a of the term ax2 and press A
  • Enter the coefficient b of the term bxy and press B
  • Enter the coefficient c of the term cyx2 and press C
  • Enter the coefficient d of the term dx and press 2nd A'
  • Enter the coefficient e of the term ex and press 2nd B'
  • Enter the independent term f and press 2nd C'
  • To start the computation press press E

The results is displayed and corresponds to the following values:

  • 0 - A point
  • 1 - Two perpendicular lines
  • 2 - Two intersecting lines
  • 3 - A single line
  • 4 - Two parallel lines
  • 5 - Nothing, no real solution
  • 6 - A circle
  • 7 - An ellipse
  • 8 - An hyperbole
  • 9 - A parabola

Then each press on R/S displays the following:

  • Theta angle between initial and rotated coordinate axes
  • Translation along X axis of origin of coordinates
  • Translation along Y axis of origin of coordinates
  • Abscises of foci
  • Ordinates of foci
  • Eccentricity
  • Term a of ax2 of standard form
  • Term b of bx (usually 0 since rotation has been performed)
  • Term c of cy2
  • Term d of dx (usually 0 since conic is translated to origin)
  • Term e of ey (usually 0 since conic is translated to origin)
  • Independent term f

After the display of the term F, pressing R/S once more, the display flashes with "9.999999 99" indicating the end of data; at that time, a program instruction erases all memories and resets the flags. Press CE to eliminate display flashing.

Formulas Used

What does this equation represent?

To find out, the following is calculated

Matrix M = (f, d÷2, e÷2; d÷2, a, b÷2; e÷2, b÷2, c)

Determinant of M = a × c × f + ( b × d × e - b2 × f - c × d2 - a × e2 ) ÷ 4

Determinant of sub matrix M11 of M = a × c - b2÷4

Determinant of sub matrix M23 of M = d × e÷4 - b × f÷2

If the determinant of M equals zero, then it is a degenerated conic :

  • If M11 is positive, it's a point
  • If M11 is negative, then if a + c equals 0, it represents two perpendicular lines; if a + c does not equal to 0, they are two intersecting lines
  • If M11 equals 0, then if M23 equals 0, then it's a single line ; if M23 does not equal to 0, they are two parallel lines

If the determinant of M is not zero, it is a conic (circle, ellipse, parabola or hyperbole):

  • If M11 is positive, then two cases:
    • If a * determinant of M is positive, no (real) solution
    • If a = c and b is zero, it is a circle, otherwise it is an ellipse
  • If M11 is negative, it is a hyperbole
  • If M11 is zero, it is a parabola

The program determines what the quadratic equation represents and then, only for non-degenerate conics, canonical or standard form is obtained through the following computations:

  • Removal of the term in xy by rotation of the coordinate axes, the angle theta is calculated between the initial set and the set in which the equation has its canonical form
  • Calculation of the translation of the origin of the coordinate axes
  • Calculate the coefficients of the standard equation of the conic

Subsequently the program calculates and displays the following:
  • Type of quadratic equation
  • Rotation angle of the coordinate axes
  • Translation in x and y of the origin of the coordinate axes
  • The x and y coordinates of the foci of the conic in the translated coordinates
  • The eccentricity of the conic section
  • The coefficients A, B, C, D, E and F of the standardized equation
